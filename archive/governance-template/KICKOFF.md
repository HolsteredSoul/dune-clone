# Kickoff (template)

> Paste the block below as the first message in a fresh agent session in this repo, after
> filling in `<<PLACEHOLDERS>>` in CONSTITUTION.md and PROJECT.md.

```
We are starting a new project. Before anything else, read CONSTITUTION.md and PROJECT.md in
the repo root and treat them as binding. They exist because projects like this drown in
complexity by repeatedly substituting clever proxies for the real objective. These docs are
the immune system against that — do not route around them.

The objective is ONE thing: <<THE ONE OBJECTIVE, in its real units, measured on held-out
reality>>. Nothing else is the objective. The moment you find yourself optimizing, gating on,
or reporting a number that is not <<the objective>>, stop and say so: you are drifting.

Do NOT write a model, a gate, or a pipeline yet. The first and only task is FEASIBILITY:
determine whether we can obtain the inputs needed to measure the objective honestly, at the
coverage and granularity required. Where an input cannot be obtained, say so plainly rather
than build around the gap. Report what exists, its coverage, cost, and gaps. Propose nothing
else until we look at the answer together.

Behavioural contract for this project:
- One objective, measured honestly on held-out reality. (CONSTITUTION.md.)
- Governing rule: no gate, metric, model term, or config flag exists unless removing it
  WORSENS the objective. Everything justifies itself in that one currency or it does not go in.
- Evidence-first: prove the result on the largest honest sample we can get BEFORE committing or
  building infrastructure around it.
- Evaluate broad, commit narrow: evaluate candidates cheaply; commit only what passes the gate.
- Be suspicious of legible proxies. They feel rigorous and they are precisely how such projects
  accrete machinery. See CONSTITUTION.md's rejected framings.
- Small is the default. Prefer deleting to adding. When unsure, do less and ask.
```
